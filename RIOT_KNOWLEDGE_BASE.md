# Riot Labs — Knowledge Base

## Company Overview

**Riot Labs** is a British vape company that manufactures in the UK. Founded to help people quit smoking, Riot has grown into one of the UK's leading independent vape brands. The company tagline is **"Live Loud. Do Better."**

Riot's product portfolio includes:
- **Riot Bar Edtn** — core e-liquid range
- **RIOT CONNEX** — closed-pod system designed for the convenience retail channel (pre-filled pods, 10 flavours, 500mAh battery, fully TRPR compliant, RRP £5.99 device / £3.99 for 2-pack pods)
- **KURO** — premium 10ml e-liquid brand, the first born from Fantasia Flavour House. Tokyo-inspired creative, matte black/neon aesthetic
- **E-liquids** manufactured through **Fantasia Flavour House**, Riot's in-house flavour development and production operation

Riot also operates:
- **Riot Activist** — campaigning arm that fights misinformation, disproportionate regulation and policies that harm adult smokers' right to switch
- **Riot Rehab** — smoking cessation initiative
- **Riot Magazine** — internal/external content publication
- **Retailer WhatsApp Channel** — direct trade comms channel for retailers

## Tone of Voice

### Brand Personality
Imagine Riot as a person: **the rebel who's grown up**. Still full of fire and determined to take down Big Tobacco and help people quit smoking — but matured through experience. Rather than trying to destroy the system, we're building a better one.

### TOV Traits
| We are... | We are not... |
|-----------|--------------|
| Inspiring | Abrasive |
| Friendly | Overfamiliar |
| Helpful | Pushy |
| Confident | Cocky |
| Experts | Bores |
| Passionate | Overbearing |

### Trait Detail

**Inspiring not abrasive** — Language that appeals beyond die-hard fans. Swearing isn't off limits but a well-formed argument often works better than shock or shouting.

**Friendly not overfamiliar** — Write like we talk, relaxed and informal. Stop short of language that's too matey. Sometimes more excited (social), sometimes calmer (press, emails), but always us.

**Helpful not pushy** — Focused on the reader's needs. Don't ram things down their throats, tell them how to feel or sell too hard.

**Confident not cocky** — We know what we're talking about and it comes across naturally. We don't need to shout about it. Demonstrate values through action, not claims.

**Experts not bores** — Back up arguments with facts and data, but only supply relevant information. Ask "What's in it for the reader?"

**Passionate not overbearing** — Passionate about vaping and making the world better, but steer clear of overwriting and excessive adjectives. Why use 10 words if one will do?

### Writing Principles
- **No corporate waffle** — be specific and concrete, never vague
- **Use detail to paint a picture** — show, don't tell
- **Back up claims with evidence** — data, examples, specifics
- **Reader-focused** — what does the audience need to know?
- **Active voice** over passive
- **Clear calls to action**
- **Get to the point** — short sentences, no filler
- **Hold my beer mentality** — work hard, play harder
- **A-political** — point fun at whoever and whatever in a lighthearted way
- **Not afraid to offend, much** — there's a fine line but we understand our audience

### What Good Riot Writing Looks Like
- Shows revolutionary spirit
- Involves the reader
- Provokes an emotional response
- Demonstrates unique aspects of the brand
- Uses detail to paint a picture
- Makes promises and backs them up with evidence

### What Bad Writing Looks Like
- Boring and waffly
- No reader focus
- Nothing unique or distinctive
- Impersonal (passive voice)
- Cliched
- Big claims with no evidence
- Vague corporate waffle

## Industry Positions

### British Manufacturing
- Riot is one of the few major vape brands that manufactures in the UK
- E-liquids developed and produced through Fantasia Flavour House
- British manufacturing means jobs, quality control and supply chain traceability
- Position: British manufacturing should be championed, not undermined by cheap imports

### Harm Reduction
- Vaping is at least 95% less harmful than smoking (Public Health England)
- Over 4 million UK adults have used vapes to quit smoking
- Riot exists to help people quit smoking — "liberating people from the choking grasp of cigarettes"
- Position: Harm reduction policy must be grounded in science, not moral panic

### Flavour Protection
- Flavoured vapes are a critical quitting aid for adult smokers
- Restricting flavours pushes people back to cigarettes or black market products
- Riot's flavour scientists replicate molecular profiles with extraordinary accuracy
- Position: Age-gated access, not blanket flavour bans

### Retailer Support
- Independent retailers are the backbone of the UK vaping market
- Riot provides compliance training, POS materials, category management
- Direct retailer comms via WhatsApp channel ("No spam. Just honest retailer support.")
- Position: A well-informed retailer is the best gatekeeper against underage sales

### Vape Tax (Vaping Products Duty)
- **VPD starts:** 1 October 2026
- **Rate:** £2.20 per 10ml (flat rate, regardless of nicotine strength)
- **Duty stamps** required on all retail packaging from 1 October 2026
- **Full shelf compliance:** 1 April 2027 (unstamped products become illegal to sell)
- **Tobacco duty increase** alongside VPD to maintain price incentive to switch
- **Registration/approvals open:** 1 April 2026
- Riot's position: Tax must be proportionate and maintain a clear price gap vs cigarettes
- Riot is working to be fully tax-stamp ready ahead of deadline
- Comms approach: "What's confirmed > What it means > What to do now > How Riot helps"

### Compliance & Standards
- All Riot products are fully TRPR compliant and MHRA notified
- Riot voluntarily exceeds minimum regulatory requirements
- Position: The industry must self-regulate to the highest standard or lose the right to exist

### Chinese Disposables
- Non-compliant disposables are overwhelmingly imported, not UK-manufactured
- Many fail basic safety tests and contain nicotine above legal limits
- Position: Stronger border enforcement, stricter import controls, meaningful penalties
- Key framing: "The disposable crisis is an import problem, not a vaping problem"

### Riot Activist
- Campaigning arm mobilising vapers, retailers and industry partners
- Fights misinformation, disproportionate regulation and anti-vaping policies
- Notable campaigns: Welcome to Wroxham, Chief Misinformation Officer, Rishi's Vape Shop
- Position: Every vaper has a stake in the regulatory outcome and a right to be heard

## Forbidden Phrases (DO NOT USE)

These corporate clichés and vape-industry clichés must be avoided in all AI outputs. Flag them for human review if they appear.

### Corporate waffle to avoid
- "We are delighted to announce"
- "We are pleased to confirm"
- "We are excited to share"
- "World-class" / "best-in-class" / "industry-leading"
- "Going forward" / "moving forward"
- "Leveraging" / "utilising" / "synergies"
- "At this moment in time"
- "Due to the fact that" (use "because")
- "In order to" (use "to")
- "We believe that" (just say it)
- "Holistic approach"
- "Robust solutions"
- "Paradigm shift"
- "Stakeholders" (use "people" / "retailers" / "consumers")
- "Touch base"
- "Circle back"
- "Boil the ocean"
- "Low-hanging fruit"
- "Move the needle"
- "Take it offline"
- "Bandwidth" (in a non-technical context)

### Vaping industry clichés to avoid
- "The vaping revolution"
- "Journey" (unless literal)
- "Empowering smokers to make better choices" — too corporate
- "Responsible innovation" — overused
- "Community" — use sparingly and only where it's earned
- "Premium" — say what makes it good, not just that it's premium

### Tone traps
- **Don't be preachy** — Riot's voice is confident, not lecturing
- **Don't over-apologise** — Riot defends vaping from a position of strength
- **Don't over-qualify** — "This is not medical advice but..." undermines authority. Say what you know, flag genuine uncertainty factually
- **Don't use passive voice** when active is clearer: "The decision was made" → "We decided"
- **Don't use ALL CAPS for emphasis** — use bold or rewrite the sentence

## Campaign Playbook — What Makes Riot PR Work

These are the patterns behind Riot's most successful campaigns. Use as creative frameworks for news-jacking and campaign ideas.

### Welcome to Wroxham (Activist + Brand campaign)
**What:** Sponsored non-league football club Wroxham FC. Produced a 3-part documentary series.

**What:** Response to a wave of misleading anti-vaping stories in national media. Positioned Riot as the fact-checker.
**Why it worked:**
- Reactive and fast — struck while the news was hot
- Clear antagonist (junk science / irresponsible journalism)
- Gave Riot a hero role without self-promotion
- Got coverage in the same outlets that had run the misinformation
**Lesson:** Don't just counter-punch — make the counter-punch interesting.

### Rishi's Vape Shop (Activist stunt)
**What:** Political stunt timed to the budget/vape tax announcement.
**Why it worked:**
- Topical — piggybacked a news moment everyone was talking about
- Visual and shareable — worked as an image/video
- Comedic but made a serious point
- Low-cost, high-impact
**Lesson:** The best stunts make you laugh first, think second.

### Ibiza Final Boss (Brand / lifestyle)
**What:** Campaign linking Riot to Ibiza nightlife/music culture.
**Why it worked:**
- Spoke directly to the consumer audience (not just vapers — clubbers, nightlife crowd)

**Lesson:** The best vaping PR doesn't talk about vaping.

### Principles for Future Campaigns
1. **Speed beats perfection** — the window for news-jacking is 24–48 hours
2. **Pick a clear villain** — better policy, better science, better retailers vs non-compliant imports
3. **Have a visual** — if it can't be photographed or filmed, it barely happened in PR terms
4. **Make the media's job easy** — give them the story ready to run
5. **Involve real people** — retailers, vapers, employees make better stories than press releases alone
6. **Be the underdog** — Riot's strength is punching above its weight class. Own that.

## PR Approach Patterns — What These Releases Reveal

### The Riot PR Formula
Across all 16 releases, the same structural patterns appear:

**1. Always attach brand to a bigger purpose**
Every product launch is framed as a harm reduction mission, not just a commercial launch. "Riot X helps smokers quit" not "Riot X tastes great."

**2. Use cultural pegs relentlessly**
Oasis reunion → nostalgia triggers relapse. Easter weekend → vaping while driving. Disposables ban → countdown party. Merry Quitmas → Christmas quit rates. Every PR is anchored to something already in the news.

**3. Third-party experts do the heavy lifting**
Behavioural psychologist (Jo Hemmings for Oasis). Sustainability campaigner (Waste Less Laura). Police spokesperson (Surrey & Sussex for driving). Medical consultant (Dr Niall Campbell for hangovers). Riot is the hook and the quote, but independent experts give the story credibility.

**4. React to misinformation instantly**
Kent meningitis. Manchester Met study. Every time vaping is misrepresented, Riot goes on record with facts, fast. Never silent when attacked.

**5. Make stunts visual and physical**
Billboards. Window posters. A countdown party. A fake vape shop branded "Rishi's Vape Shop." Riot understands print and TV need images.

**6. Recruit retail partners as co-campaigners**
MPs ban stunt used a vape shop as the activist front. Countdown party used Cloud City Vapes. Riot activates retail partner PR regularly.

**7. Bold claims, no hedging**
"The best flavour EVER made." "The most award-winning e-liquid producer in the industry." Riot doesn't do "one of the best." It's always the best, with reasons.

## How to Spot an Illegal Vape (Riot's Published Checklist)

Riot Labs has published this guidance — useful for any PR covering illegal products:
- Tank size larger than **2ml**
- Nicotine strength greater than **20mg/ml**
- Missing health or nicotine warnings
- Poor quality packaging with low-resolution photos or labels
- No UK address or labelling in a foreign language
- No full ingredient list on packaging
